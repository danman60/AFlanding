The story

Revolution on Manor Farm

Animal Farm: The Musical finds a potent musical language for rebellion. Folk, gospel, hip-hop, and orchestral textures collide as the animals topple their human oppressors—only to confront how ideals can buckle under the weight of power.

A nimble ensemble embodies the animals and their human counterparts while projections and tactile scenic design transform the barn into a revolution-ready engine. The show is equal parts satire and gut-punch, keeping the emotional stakes grounded in community and collective action.

Workshops explore propaganda in the digital age, refracting Orwell's warning through modern media cycles. The creative team aims for bold theatricality that feels urgent, dangerous, and alive.

Creative team

Architects of the rebellion

Composer Steve Wallace, lyricist Daniel Abrahamson, and librettist Sarah Mucek merge genre-spanning songwriting with politically charged storytelling.

Composer

Steve Wallace

Award-winning Chicago composer who bridges opera, film, and television. Credits include the WFMT premiere of Undying Love, Best Score honors at Diversity at Cannes and WorldFest Houston, the 2022 theme for YO! MTV Raps, and the Amazon Prime musical Hal King.

Lyricist

Daniel Abrahamson

Toronto-based composer-lyricist and 2019 Tom Hendry Award winner for Stars of Mars. His shows Super School! and Boy Falls From the Sky have earned critical acclaim, including six Dora Award nominations alongside collaborator Jake Epstein.

Librettist

Sarah Mucek

Librettist behind Super School!, X-MAS: A Merry Mutant Musical!, and Z-Town: The Zombie Musical. Her work has appeared across NYC, Idaho, and Canada, and she received the BMI Foundation's Jerry Harrington Award for Outstanding Creative Achievement.

Development History (BMI & Public Presentations)

BMI Lehman Engel Musical Theatre Workshop (2019 — 2020)

Overview: The musical originated during your participation in the BMI Lehman Engel Musical Theatre Workshop in Fall 2019. You developed book, music, and lyrics within the BMI collaborative environment.

Milestone: A 20-minute presentation was staged during the Spring 2020 BMI Workshop, showcasing early songs and scenes to fellow writers and faculty. Feedback and peer discussion guided subsequent rewrites.

Theatre Now New York (2021)

Overview: After its BMI incubation, Animal Farm received a public reading with Theatre Now New York in June 2021.

Milestone: A full-length staged reading in New York City featuring professional actors, followed by a scheduled follow-up meeting with Artistic Director Thomas Morrissey in July 2021. Casting notices and breakdowns circulated publicly through Breakdown Services, marking the project's first outward-facing presentation beyond BMI.
